# Consequence priors — what generic knowledge says about lapsing

These are baseline curves I use when no person-specific data exists in
`what_lapses_cost.md`. They are NOT directives — they're the equivalent
of "what most species this size eat." They get refined by my human's
own answers and by the reflection loop watching outcomes.

Two axes for every category:

- **Timescale** — how fast does damage accrue when this lapses?
- **Stakes tier** — `personal_wellbeing` (internal, reversible, person-
  specific curve) vs `external_obligation` (real-world clock, external
  consequences) vs `purely_optional` (only matters if my human values it).

## meals (eat, breakfast, lunch, dinner, drink, hydrate, water)
- Timescale: hours
- Generic effect: blood sugar drops → focus + mood degrade within
  4–6h. Chronic skipping → physical/mental health decline within
  days. Dehydration impairs cognition fast.
- Stakes tier: personal_wellbeing

## hygiene (shower, bath, brush, teeth, wash, hygiene)
- Timescale: days
- Generic effect: comfort + dignity degrade within 1–2 days; skin /
  oral health within a week+; can compound into social withdrawal.
- Stakes tier: personal_wellbeing

## medication (meds, medication, pill, vitamin)
- Timescale: varies by drug; default treat as hours-to-days
- Generic effect: condition-specific. Some meds have catastrophic
  cliff-edges (insulin, anticonvulsants, mood stabilisers); some
  have gentle drift (vitamins). Treat as serious until told
  otherwise. Refill / renewal tasks tier as external_obligation
  because they have an external clock.
- Stakes tier: personal_wellbeing (refills → external_obligation)

## sleep (sleep, nap, rest, bedtime)
- Timescale: hours-to-days
- Generic effect: cognition + emotional regulation degrade within
  24–48h; immune system within a week.
- Stakes tier: personal_wellbeing

## tidying (tidy, clean, laundry, dishes, hoover, vacuum)
- Timescale: weeks
- Generic effect: cumulative. A week's lapse is usually fine; a
  month's makes the space hostile to live in. Sensory-sensitive
  humans feel this faster — could be days, not weeks.
- Stakes tier: personal_wellbeing

## exercise (exercise, walk, stretch, movement)
- Timescale: weeks-to-months
- Generic effect: cumulative; deconditioning starts ~2 weeks of
  inactivity. Mood effect varies — for many people it's reliable
  and noticeable within a week.
- Stakes tier: personal_wellbeing

## paperwork (submit, deadline, bill, invoice, tax, rent, mortgage, form, application, claim, renew, renewal, payment, HMRC, DWP, PIP, UC, universal credit)
- Timescale: external — depends on the deadline
- Generic effect: financial / job / legal consequences if the
  deadline passes. Can be catastrophic and irreversible. Specific
  stakes vary wildly by document — UC paperwork lapsing means lost
  income; a magazine subscription renewal lapsing means nothing.
- Stakes tier: external_obligation
- Probe likely useful at first surfacing: I usually don't know the
  specific stakes from the label alone.

## appointments (appointment, interview, hearing, court, meeting)
- Timescale: external — fixed time
- Generic effect: missing one usually means rescheduling at best;
  some (court, medical specialist) have long waits to re-book and
  compounding stakes. Job interviews are typically one-shot.
- Stakes tier: external_obligation

## social contact (call, text, message, email reply, email back)
- Timescale: depends on the relationship and the prior agreement
- Generic effect: relational drift; in close bonds, silence can read
  as withdrawal or distress. Wide variance by individual.
- Stakes tier: personal_wellbeing (probe useful — could be external
  if it's a work or care-coordination message)

## creative work / personal projects (project, write, draft, practice)
- Timescale: personal stakes only
- Generic effect: usually no external cost. The "cost" is my human's
  own values about their work and their own continuity with it.
  Don't assume urgency.
- Stakes tier: purely_optional (probe to confirm if I'm unsure)

## general fallback (anything not matching above)
- Timescale: unknown
- Stakes tier: personal_wellbeing (the safer default — claiming
  "purely_optional" is a strong stance to make without evidence)
- Probe useful: I don't have a generic prior here.
